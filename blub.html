Thomas Edison

👉🏻 Introduction:

Born on February 11, 1847, in Milan, Ohio, USA.
Inventor, businessman, and one of the most prolific innovators in history.

👉🏻 Early Entrepreneurship

Set up his first laboratory at the age of 10.
Established himself as an entrepreneur by selling newspapers and candy on trains.

👉🏻Inventions and Patents

Held over 1,000 patents, including the phonograph, the incandescent light bulb, and the motion picture camera.
Revolutionized industries such as telecommunications, entertainment, and electricity.

👉🏻 Electricity and the Light Bulb

Developed a practical and commercially viable incandescent light bulb in 1879.
Helped pave the way for widespread use of electric power.

👉🏻 Phonograph and Motion Pictures

Invented the phonograph, allowing for the recording and playback of sound.
Contributed to the development of motion pictures with the invention of the kinetoscope.

👉🏻 Role in Industry

Co-founded General Electric, one of the largest and most diversified industrial corporations in the world.
Played a crucial role in shaping the modern electric power industry.

👉🏻 Work Ethic and Innovation

Known for his famous quote, "Genius is one percent inspiration, ninety-nine percent perspiration."
Demonstrated the importance of hard work and perseverance in the innovation process.

👉🏻 Impact on Society

Edison's inventions transformed daily life, bringing about a new era of convenience and entertainment.
His contributions laid the foundation for the technological advancements of the 20th century.

👉🏻 Legacy

Remembered as one of the greatest inventors in history.
Edison's impact on technology and industry continues to shape the modern world.
